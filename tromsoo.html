Tromsø AI-scandal

AI-Generated Fake Sources Spark Scandal in Tromsø

Tromsø Municipality has found itself in a scandal after using artificial intelligence (AI) to generate sources in its proposal for a new kindergarten and school structure, which includes closing eight schools. Out of 18 listed sources, only seven could be verified. Among the fabricated references was a 2018 book titled "Quality in Schools: Learning, Well-being, and Relationships" by Professor Thomas Nordahl, which does not exist. Nordahl expressed surprise at being cited for something he never wrote.

Municipality Halts Proposal Amid Trust Concerns

Municipal Director Stig Tore Johnsen admitted that AI was used as a tool and has decided to halt the consultation process for six months to review the errors. He emphasized the importance of trust in decision-making and apologized for the serious mistakes. Researcher Jonas Stein described the incident as Norway's first major AI scandal in the public sector, highlighting the risks of relying on AI-generated data without proper quality control.

Summary

    - Tromsø Municipality used AI-generated sources in a school restructuring proposal, with 11 out of 18 sources being fake.

    - A non-existent book by Professor Thomas Nordahl was falsely cited, causing public outcry.

    - The proposal is now paused for six months to address the errors and restore trust.
