Alphabet's Waymo aims for 2026 self-driving ride-hailing launch in Washington, D.C

Expansion to Washington, D.C

Waymo plans to launch its driverless ride-hailing service in Washington, D.C., despite current laws requiring a human driver. The company is working with policymakers to establish the legal framework needed for fully autonomous operations. Given D.C.'s role as a regulatory hub, this expansion could influence self-driving laws nationwide.

High Ride Volume & Growth

Waymo One already provides 200,000+ weekly paid trips in cities like San Francisco, Phoenix, and Los Angeles, completing over 4 million trips in 2024 alone. The company is expanding to Atlanta, Miami, and Washington, D.C., signaling growing demand for autonomous taxis.

Regulatory and Safety Concerns

Despite its success, Waymo faces scrutiny from the NHTSA, which is investigating reports of its vehicles failing to avoid obstacles. Past issues include a wooden pole collision in Phoenix and a 444-vehicle recall due to a software error. These incidents highlight the ongoing challenges of ensuring self-driving safety.
